What is it?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think and learn like humans. These systems can perform tasks such as recognizing speech, making decisions, translating languages, and more. AI is a broad field that encompasses various subfields, including machine learning, neural networks, and natural language processing.

How does artificial intelligence work?

AI systems work by using algorithms and large datasets to identify patterns and make predictions or decisions. Machine learning, a subset of AI, involves training models on data so they can improve over time. Neural networks, inspired by the human brain, are a key technology in AI that enable deep learning. Key components include data collection, algorithm selection, model training, and evaluation.

What are the types?

AI has 7 classifications that are determined by two points: its capacity and its technical classification.

In terms of its capacity, this relates to the level of intelligence of the AI, meaning its ability to perform human-like functions. These abilities are divided into four categories:

  1. Reactive Machines: These are the oldest forms of artificial intelligence, which do not have functionality based on memory.
  2. Limited Memory: These can learn based on historical data.
  3. Theory of Mind:This is the next level of AI systems that is currently in development.
  4. Self-aware: Self-aware AI is a hypothetical formulation that will be able to understand and evoke emotions, needs, beliefs, and potentially its own desires.

Now, regarding the technical classification of artificial intelligence, we should focus on three categories:

  1. Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI): This represents all existing AI, which can only perform a specific task.
  2. Artificial General Intelligence (AGI): This refers to the capability of general artificial intelligence to learn, perceive, understand, and function completely like a human being.
  3. Artificial Superintelligence (ASI): This can replicate the multifaceted intelligence of humans, has greater memory, analyzes data quickly, and possesses decision-making capabilities.

Where is it present?

AI is integrated into various sectors and everyday applications:

  1. Healthcare: Diagnosis, treatment plans, robotic surgeries.
  2. Finance: Fraud detection, trading algorithms, customer service.
  3. Automotive: Self-driving cars, predictive maintenance.
  4. Retail: Personalized recommendations, inventory management.
  5. Entertainment: Content recommendations, video game AI.
  6. Home: Smart assistants like Alexa and Google Home.

Advantages and Disadvantages

Like any innovation, artificial intelligence also has its advantages and disadvantages. Below is a list of them:

Advantages:
  • Efficiency and automation of tasks.
  • Data-driven decision making.
  • Reduction of human error.
  • New product and service innovation.
Disadvantages:
  • Job displacement and economic impact.
  • Ethical concerns and bias in AI decisions.
  • Security risks and misuse of AI.
  • High costs of implementation and maintenance.

History of artificial intelligence

The automation of manual tasks and the replication of human consciousness have always been topics in scientific debate, predating even the invention of computers. Studies on neural networks and the methods by which a machine can perform tasks like the human brain gained momentum in the 20th century, definitively expanding from the 1950s onwards.

One of the key figures in science and considered the father of computing, Alan Turing (1912-1954), wrote one of the most comprehensive studies of the period on the subject. The article "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" was published in 1950 and was the first to mention the term "artificial intelligence." In it, Turing proposed a test to evaluate whether machines have the ability to emulate human thought and pass as a person to the point of confusing questioners, which became known as the "Turing Test."

In the subsequent period, many studies and research projects were developed to test Turing's proposal, with a highlight being the program Eliza, created by computer scientist Joseph Weizenbaum (1923-2008) in 1966, which achieved relative success but also faced considerable criticism. Before that, researchers at Stanford University in California (United States) launched in 1965 what is considered the pioneering system in artificial intelligence, called Dendral.

Since then, many advances have been made in the field of computer science, robotics, and artificial intelligence, to the point where these concepts and elements are part of our daily lives. In the more recent period, the emergence of algorithms capable of directly interacting with humans through language and dialogue development, like Chat GPT, and others capable of generating images with just a few commands, has sparked intense debate in society about the role of artificial intelligence and the limits of these tools.

Will AI steal your job?!

The future of AI points towards and urges for increasingly transparent technology, ethically built, and integrated into daily tasks, whether at work or in our personal lives, enhancing our cognitive abilities.

AI can make humans more productive, freeing professionals from certain mechanical and repetitive tasks so they can use their full capacity to create and innovate in other sectors. A change in the job market has begun, and certainly, this will eliminate some jobs in the future.

But speaking from an optimistic perspective, Alessandro Jannuzzi, Director of Engineering and Innovation at Microsoft Brazil, states that the company has committed to democratizing access to AI. “We are looking for the necessary resources in AI to help solve the most urgent problems in our society. In this sense, our approach is divided into three pillars: leading innovations that enhance human capacity; building powerful platforms that make innovation faster and more accessible; and developing a reliable approach that puts the customer in control and protects their data,” he explained.

It is worth noting that in the business world, if you are not being disruptive in your sector, certainly someone else is. And AI can be the greatest ally in contributing to the kind of digital transformation that companies seek so much. Combined with human capacity, it can drive people to do amazing things.
